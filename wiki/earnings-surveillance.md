## THE BIG 3: JULY 29–30 EARNINGS GAUNTLET

> *The most consequential earnings reports for the entire market. MSFT, META, AAPL, and AMZN together represent ~20% of the S&P 500. When they move, the market moves. Consensus refreshed 2026-07-18 via Yahoo Finance.*

### Microsoft (MSFT) — Wednesday, July 29, After Close

**Consensus EPS:** $4.24 (range $4.07–$4.89) | **Consensus Revenue:** $87.7B (range $86.9B–$91.2B)

**What the Council is Watching:**
- **Azure growth:** Consensus 39–40% YoY. Above 40% = bull. Below 37% = bear.
- **AI ARR:** Is Microsoft's AI business still accelerating? The market wants acceleration, not deceleration.
- **Cloud margin:** Azure gross margins have been expanding. Any compression signals price wars with AWS and Google Cloud.
- **Guidance:** Next-quarter revenue guidance. The bar is ~$90B+. Anything below $88B would be a shock.

**Marky's Binary Read:** MSFT is the most important earnings report of the week. A beat with strong Azure guidance launches XLK toward new highs. A miss on Azure growth triggers a 5–8% drop and drags the entire tech complex down.

### Meta Platforms (META) — Wednesday, July 29, After Close

**Consensus EPS:** $7.23–$7.39 (Yahoo sources differ; range $6.46–$8.55) | **Consensus Revenue:** $60.3B (range $59.0B–$62.8B)

**What the Council is Watching:**
- **Ad revenue growth:** Consensus ~+18% YoY. The ad cycle is turning — is Meta leading or lagging?
- **AI capex:** The market expects $60B+ in 2026 AI capex. Accelerating or moderating?
- **Llama monetization:** Is the open-source AI strategy generating enterprise revenue?
- **Reels vs. TikTok:** Reels engagement and monetization. Is Meta winning the short-form war?

**Cecil's Fundamental Read:** Meta's ad business is a cash machine — $40B+ in annual free cash flow. The AI capex is the wild card — moat or money pit? If Meta reports $7.50+ EPS and guides $8.00+, the stock breaks out. If ad revenue decelerates, it drops hard. Note the wide analyst range ($6.46–$8.55) — the street itself is uncertain.

### Apple (AAPL) — Thursday, July 30, After Close

**Consensus EPS:** $1.89 (range $1.83–$1.99) | **Consensus Revenue:** $108.9B (range $107.5B–$112.2B)

**What the Council is Watching:**
- **iPhone revenue:** Is the Apple Intelligence upgrade cycle real?
- **Services revenue:** The margin engine. Any deceleration is a red flag.
- **China revenue:** ~18% of revenue. Geopolitics and Huawei competition are headwinds.
- **Guidance:** The market wants iPhone AI momentum confirmed.

**Ophelia's Macro Read:** Apple is the consumer bellwether. A beat with strong iPhone guidance = healthy consumer. A miss on China and iPhone = weakening consumer. The Fed is watching Apple as closely as the market is.

### Amazon (AMZN) — Thursday, July 30, After Close

**Consensus EPS:** $1.82 (range $1.56–$2.10) | **Consensus Revenue:** $195.9B (range $186.0B–$200.1B)

**What the Council is Watching:** AWS growth (the margin engine), retail margins, advertising revenue, and capex guidance. AMZN has the widest EPS range of the mega-caps — the street cannot agree on retail profitability.

### Also on the Big-Cap Radar

- **Alphabet (GOOGL)** — Wednesday, **July 22**. Consensus EPS $2.89–$2.90, revenue ~$116.9B. Search + Cloud + AI monetization. Reports a full week before the other mega-caps — it sets the tone for the entire gauntlet.
- **Tesla (TSLA)** — Wednesday, **July 22**. Consensus EPS $0.54 (wide range $0.31–$0.74), revenue ~$26.4B. Deliveries, FSD, margins. The meme stock wildcard.
- **NVIDIA (NVDA)** — **August 26** (outside the 2-week window). Early consensus EPS $2.09 (range $2.03–$2.60), revenue ~$91.8B. The final boss of earnings season. Mark it on the calendar.
