## THE BIG 3: JULY 29–30 EARNINGS GAUNTLET

> *The three most consequential earnings reports for the entire market. These three companies represent ~15% of the S&P 500 and ~25% of the NASDAQ. When they move, the market moves.*

### Microsoft (MSFT) — July 29, After Close

**Consensus EPS:** $4.24 | **Consensus Revenue:** $69.2B

**What the Council is Watching:**
- **Azure growth:** Consensus 39–40% YoY. Above 40% = bull. Below 37% = bear.
- **AI ARR:** Is Microsoft's AI business above $40B annualized? The market wants to see acceleration, not deceleration.
- **Cloud margin:** Azure gross margins have been expanding. Any compression would signal price wars with AWS and Google Cloud.
- **Guidance:** Q1 FY2027 revenue guidance. The market expects $70B+. Anything below $68B would be a shock.

**Marky's Binary Read:** MSFT is the most important earnings report of the week. A beat with strong Azure guidance recaptures XLK's 50D MA and launches the sector toward $195. A miss on Azure growth triggers a 5–8% drop and drags the entire tech complex down.

### Meta Platforms (META) — July 29, After Close

**Consensus EPS:** $7.20 | **Consensus Revenue:** $42.8B

**What the Council is Watching:**
- **Ad revenue growth:** Consensus +18% YoY. The ad cycle is turning — is Meta leading or lagging?
- **AI capex:** The market expects $60B+ in 2026 AI capex. Is the spending accelerating or moderating?
- **Llama monetization:** Is Meta's open-source AI strategy generating enterprise revenue?
- **Reels vs. TikTok:** Reels engagement and monetization metrics. Is Meta winning the short-form video war?

**Cecil's Fundamental Read:** Meta at 24.8x P/E is a growth stock, not a value play. But the ad business is a cash machine — $40B+ in annual free cash flow. The AI capex is a wild card — is it a moat or a money pit? If Meta reports $7.50+ EPS and guides $8.00+, the stock breaks above $700. If ad revenue decelerates, the stock drops to $600.

### Apple (AAPL) — July 30, After Close

**Consensus EPS:** $1.89 | **Consensus Revenue:** $108–110B

**What the Council is Watching:**
- **iPhone revenue:** The iPhone 16 cycle is the first with Apple Intelligence. Is the AI upgrade cycle real?
- **Services revenue:** The $90B+ annual services business is the margin engine. Any deceleration is a red flag.
- **China revenue:** China is 18% of Apple's revenue. Geopolitical tensions and competition from Huawei are headwinds.
- **Guidance:** Tim Cook's last earnings call as CEO (if the succession rumors are true). The market wants to see iPhone AI momentum.

**Ophelia's Macro Read:** Apple is the consumer bellwether. If AAPL beats with strong iPhone guidance, the consumer is healthy. If AAPL misses on China revenue and iPhone sales, the consumer is weakening. The Fed is watching Apple as closely as the market is.
