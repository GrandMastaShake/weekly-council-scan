**Sector Bifurcation:** XLRE is not your father's real estate fund. It is split into four distinct groups:

**The Logistics Giants (The E-Commerce Play):**
- PLD (Prologis) — the world's largest logistics REIT. Warehouses, distribution centers, last-mile facilities. E-commerce = demand for industrial space. PLD is the Amazon supply chain in REIT form.

**The Cell Towers (The Connectivity Play):**
- AMT, CCI, SBAC — cell tower REITs. 5G rollout = more towers, more tenants, more revenue. These are infrastructure plays with long-term leases. AMT is the global leader. CCI is the U.S. leader (fiber sale closing H1 2026 is THE catalyst). SBAC is the pure-play tower REIT.

**The Data Centers (The AI Play):**
- EQIX, DLR — data center REITs. AI = massive demand for data center space. Hyperscalers are leasing every square foot. EQIX is the interconnection leader. DLR is the hyperscale leader. These are the fastest-growing REITs — and the most correlated to the tech tape.

**The Traditional REITs (The Bond Proxies):**
- PSA (self-storage), O (triple-net retail), WELL (senior housing/healthcare), SPG (malls) — traditional REITs with stable cash flows but lower growth. O is the "Monthly Dividend Company" — a dividend aristocrat. WELL is a healthcare REIT benefiting from aging demographics. SPG is the mall REIT that survived the retail apocalypse.

**The PLD Story:** Prologis delivered the week. Q2 results on **Jul 16 beat**: Core FFO of **$1.63 (+6.5% surprise)**, record leasing, and management **raised 2026 guidance for the second time**. The stock ripped +6.3% on the week to $149.79 — 2.3% below its $153.35 high, RSI 69. The logistics cycle that everyone feared was rolling over just re-accelerated. At 36x P/E the market is paying up, but this is what leadership looks like: the largest REIT in the fund making highs on fundamentals, not rate hopes.

**The WELL Story:** Welltower at $243.25, +5.0% this week, **+32.0% YTD**, closed the week a whisper under its new $246.43 high. The demographic wave (10,000 boomers turning 65 daily) is now showing up in occupancy and rent growth, and WELL has become the sector's momentum king — RSI 69. At 112x P/E and a 1.25% yield it remains a growth stock wearing a REIT costume, but the market has stopped caring: this is the best-performing major REIT of 2026.

**The SPG Story:** Simon Property Group at $228.70, +4.5% this week, +26.4% YTD, sitting 1.2% under its $231.53 high. The premium-mall thesis keeps defying the obituary writers.
