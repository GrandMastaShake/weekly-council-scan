**Ophelia Verdict:** The yield curve is steepening — this is the **single most important factor** for financials. When the curve steepens, banks borrow short and lend long, expanding NIM. When it inverts or flattens, NIM compresses. The curve went from deeply inverted (-50 bps) to mildly positive (+26 bps) in six months. This is a **regime change** for banks. But the steepening is fragile. If the Fed hikes in September, short rates rise faster than long rates, and the curve flattens again. If the Fed cuts, the curve steepens further. The banks are hostage to the Fed's next move. I am watching the 2Y–10Y spread like a hawk.

**The CRE overhang:** Commercial real estate prices are down ~15% from peak. Regional banks (PNC, USB, TFC) hold the bulk of CRE exposure. This is the **slow-motion crisis** that never quite detonates but never quite resolves. Every quarterly earnings season, the market asks: *how much more CRE reserve build?* The answer so far has been *less than feared.* But the risk is not gone. It is just hibernating.

**The Bottom Line:** Financials is a **stock-picker's sector** right now. The money centers and investment banks are breaking out. The super-regionals are breaking down. The regionals are treading water. XLF's flat YTD (-1.22%) masks a massive internal dispersion. This is not a sector to own passively. It is a sector to trade actively.

- Regulatory overhang intensifies (Basel III endgame) → capital requirements rise → ROE compresses

### ⚠️ The Wild Card
The **Basel III endgame** rules are being finalized. If the Fed/Treasury impose higher capital requirements on the largest banks (JPM, BAC, WFC, C), their ROE could compress by 100–200 bps. This is a **stealth tax** on the money center banks that the market is not fully pricing. If the rules are stricter than expected, JPM at 16x P/E becomes expensive fast. If they are watered down, the banks get a relief rally.
